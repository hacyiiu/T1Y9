Café Terrace at Night
Vincent van Gogh

Café Terrace at Night is one of the most famous works of art by Dutch artist Vincent Van Gogh painted in 1888. This oil painting looks simple, but there may be a hidden meaning attached. Café Terrace at Night is the first part of the trilogy of Starry Night paintings that Van Gogh painted during this period. Café Terrace at Night was painted the same month as Starry Night over the Rhone, the second painting in the trilogy. Van Gogh revisited the subject of the starry night once more nearly a year later in June 1889 with Starry Night, his acclaimed masterpiece. He manages to subtly relate the magnificent painting to the old Parisian Cafe culture and lifestyle of his time. Representing the biblical Messiah as an ordinary, anonymous waiter in an outdoor street cafe, his painting suggests it is to glimpse the element of beauty. For Van Gogh, ordinary things are truly amazing, especially at night under a starry sky.
