Flower Mantango
Takashi Murakami

Takashi Murakami's Matango flower is majestic and elegant yet tacky and frightening. At the center of the statue is Murakami's iconic flower ball, containing a smiling daisy in 3D relief, with a unique candy color number. From above the sphere is a configuration of vines and tendrils, a path is generated, symbolizing Murakami's high level of craftsmanship. The title references directly from the 1963 Japanese horror film Matango which featured a mushroom-headed monster. The Menyango Flowers were exhibited in the gilded halls of the Palace of Versailles in Paris in 2009 a testament to the power of Murakami's provocative works in blending high and low art and culture. By engaging in socio-cultural surveillance through a cute and sweet kawaii aesthetic, Flower Matango is positioned at the pinnacle of Murakami's practice and iconography.
